About

The Leeds Astronomical Society was successfully founded in 1859, this was the earliest in the UK in the discipline. One of the main founders was 15-year-old William Trant, son of a family belonging to the Leeds “Phil & Lit society”. We take pride in our history as it represents our passion for discovering more about our world. From 1940 to 1946, all Society meetings were suspended due to the war.

On 23rd June 1984 the Society celebrated its 125th anniversary with a one day Symposium at the University. More recently the Society's full day ASTROMEET gathering has become a popular annual event on the amateur astronomy scene, and has gained a national reputation for being the finest event of its kind held in the north of England.
